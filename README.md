# CRM-SALES-POWER-BI-DASHBOARD
📊 CRM Sales Opportunities Analysis — Power BI Dashboard
Author: Asad Ali
A comprehensive Business Intelligence analysis of a B2B CRM sales pipeline using Power BI. This project explores sales performance, win rates, regional trends, product insights, and revenue forecasting across a fictional company's customer relationship management system.

📁 Dataset
Source: Maven Analytics — CRM Sales Opportunities Dataset
The dataset simulates a real-world B2B sales environment and consists of 5 interconnected tables:
TableDescriptionAccountsCustomer company details — sector, revenue, employees, locationProductsProduct catalogue with series (GTX, MG, GTK) and pricingSales TeamsSales reps, managers, and regional office assignmentsSales PipelineOpportunity tracking across deal stages (Prospecting → Won/Lost)Data DictionaryMetadata definitions for all fields

🎯 Project Objectives

Analyse sales performance across reps, regions, and sectors
Calculate win rates and identify factors that drive deal success
Identify top-performing products and customer segments
Detect seasonal trends and forecast future revenue
Surface cross-selling opportunities to maximise deal value

💡 Key Insights
🏆 Product Performance

The GTX family dominates across all regions, with GTX Plus Pro generating the highest revenue from won deals.
Product families ranked by deal frequency: GTX (4 products) > MG (2 products) > GTK (1 product).

🌍 Regional Performance

The West region leads in win rate (0.64) and net profit ($3.21M).
Sales reps are evenly distributed: East (12), West (12), Central (11).
Top sales rep: Darcel Schlecht — 37% of deals won.

🏭 Sector Insights

Retail has the most customer companies (17), followed by Medical and Technology (12 each).
Entertainment generates the highest average revenue per deal ($2.7K).
Tech and Retail sectors show the most room to improve prospecting-to-engaging conversion.

📅 Seasonal Trends

Sales peak in March and June; slower periods in April and October.
Revenue forecast shows steady performance with peaks in July–August.

🔁 Deal Stage Progression

Prospecting → Engaging conversion: 76%
Engaging → Closed conversion: 81%
Technology and Retail benefit most from targeted outreach at the prospecting stage.


✅ Recommendations

Focus on Retail, Technology, and Medical sectors — highest customer concentration and growth potential.
Replicate West region best practices across East and Central teams.
Launch cross-sell campaigns targeting multi-product customers, who generate higher average deal values.
Double down on GTX Plus Pro — highest revenue generator with strong market demand.
Improve prospecting conversion in Technology and Retail with personalised outreach.
Align campaigns with seasonal peaks (March, June) and use slower months for lead nurturing.
Enhance forecasting models with product-level and customer behaviour variables.
Invest in retention programmes for newer customer cohorts (acquired 2017+).
Reallocate resources to the West region while improving East region efficiency.
Use product–sector combinations (e.g. GTX in Tech, MG in Retail) to target high-conversion pairings.
